# The Battle Plan — Alcohol Reduction Strategy

## Mission Statement

Reduce alcohol consumption to **3 alcohol-free (AF) days per week**: Tuesday, Thursday, and Sunday. Replace drinking occasions with exercise and productive activities. Build momentum toward expanding AF days as confidence grows.

> "There is no tomorrow. There is only the next 10 minutes."

## The 6 Strategies

### 1. Calendar X's
Mark every completed AF day with a bold X on the calendar. The visual streak creates psychological momentum — you don't want to break the chain. Each X is proof that the old pattern doesn't own you.

### 2. Remove Alcohol from the Home
Don't keep alcohol in the house on AF days. Remove the option entirely. Willpower is finite; environment design is permanent. Make the right choice the easy choice.

### 3. The 10-Minute Contract
When an urge hits, commit to **10 minutes of anything else**. Pushups, a walk, call someone, play a game. The urge will pass. It always does. You just need to outlast it.

### 4. The Liquor Store Protocol
Driving home past a liquor store? **Drive past. Don't stop.** The 30 seconds of driving past is the ENTIRE battle. Text your accountability partner instead. The moment of decision is 30 seconds long — survive that, and you've won.

### 5. Script the Rationalizations
Your brain will generate lies. Know them in advance. Have the counter ready BEFORE the lie appears.

### 6. Stack Dopamine
Alcohol provides dopamine. Replace it with other dopamine sources: exercise, cold showers, favorite meals, gaming, music, social connection. Have the replacement **ready and planned** before the urge hits.

## The Rationalization Playbook

Your brain is a world-class rationalizer, especially with ADHD in the mix. These are the 5 lies and their counters:

| # | The Lie | The Counter |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | **"I'll start tomorrow"** ⏳ | Tomorrow doesn't exist. There is only the next 10 minutes. What are you doing RIGHT NOW? |
| 2 | **"I've been good, I earned this"** 🏆 | You earned MOMENTUM. A drink doesn't reward you — it resets you. The reward IS the streak. |
| 3 | **"It's just one"** ☝️ | When has it ever been just one? Be honest with yourself. "Just one" is the opening line of every slip story. |
| 4 | **"I'm bored, nothing to do"** 😑 | Boredom is a signal, not a sentence. 20 pushups. A walk. Call someone. Move your body. |
| 5 | **"This isn't that serious"** 🙄 | If it wasn't serious, you wouldn't have built a whole system to fight it. Respect the mission. |

## Daily Protocol for AF Days

### Morning (7:00 - 8:00 AM)
- [ ] Acknowledge: "Today is an AF day"
- [ ] Review your streak count
- [ ] Plan your evening replacement activity (gym, walk, game, etc.)
- [ ] Set a reminder for 5:00 PM danger zone

### Afternoon (5:00 PM — The Danger Zone)
- [ ] Activate Liquor Store Protocol
- [ ] Execute your planned replacement activity
- [ ] If urges hit: 10-Minute Contract
- [ ] Text accountability partner

### Evening (8:00 - 9:00 PM)
- [ ] Check in: Did you hold the line? YES or NO
- [ ] Log the result on HealthierMe dashboard
- [ ] If YES: Celebrate. Feel the streak growing.
- [ ] If NO: No shame. Investigate the trigger. Log the data.

### Bedtime
- [ ] Mark the X on the calendar
- [ ] Preview tomorrow's plan
- [ ] "I am someone who keeps promises to myself"

## Accountability Check-in Format

Every AF day evening, answer these 3 questions:

1. **Did you hold the line?** (YES / NO)
2. **What was the hardest moment?** (Identify the trigger)
3. **What rationalization tried to get through?** (Name the lie)

## Progression Plan

- **Level 1 (Current):** 3 AF days/week — Tue, Thu, Sun
- **Level 2:** 4 AF days/week — add Monday
- **Level 3:** 5 AF days/week — add Wednesday
- **Level 4:** Weekdays AF, weekends flexible
- **Level 5:** Full control — drink only when you choose to, not when the pattern chooses for you
